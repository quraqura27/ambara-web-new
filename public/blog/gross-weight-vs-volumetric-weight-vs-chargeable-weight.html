Gross Weight vs Volumetric Weight vs Chargeable Weight

A
Ambara Artha Team
June 21, 2026

Gross weight, volumetric weight, and chargeable weight are three different weight terms used in air freight quotation and airline billing. They look similar, but they answer different questions.

Simple rule: gross weight is the physical weight, volumetric weight is the space-based weight, and chargeable weight is the higher number used for pricing.

What is Gross Weight?

Gross weight is the actual physical weight of the cargo, including product, carton, inner packing, pallet, and any other export packing. If a box weighs 35 kg on a scale, the gross weight is 35 kg.

What is Volumetric Weight?

Volumetric weight is calculated from cargo dimensions. In air freight, a common formula is:

Length (cm) × Width (cm) × Height (cm) ÷ 6000 = Volumetric Weight (kg)

The divisor may vary by airline, route, product, or service type, so it should always be confirmed before booking.

What is Chargeable Weight?

Chargeable weight is the billing weight. The airline or forwarder compares gross weight and volumetric weight, then uses the higher value.

Example

ItemValue
Carton size80 × 60 × 50 cm
Gross weight30 kg
Volumetric weight80 × 60 × 50 ÷ 6000 = 40 kg
Chargeable weight40 kg

Even though the cargo physically weighs 30 kg, the quote may be based on 40 kg because the box takes more aircraft space than its actual weight suggests.

Common Mistake

A common mistake is sending only gross weight when asking for an air freight quotation. Without dimensions, the first quote may be inaccurate. When the cargo is measured at warehouse or by the airline, the chargeable weight may increase.

Why It Matters for Indonesia Shipments

For cargo moving to or from Indonesia, chargeable weight affects air freight cost, warehouse planning, pickup vehicle planning, and sometimes the practicality of using air freight instead of sea freight. This is especially important for bulky but light cargo, e-commerce cartons, samples, display items, garments, packaging materials, and personal effects.

Checklist Before Requesting a Quote

  • Number of packages
  • Gross weight per package
  • Length, width, and height of each package
  • Stackability and pallet details, if any
  • Origin and destination airport or address
  • Commodity description
  • Expected ready date

FAQ

Is chargeable weight always higher than gross weight?

No. It is the higher of gross weight and volumetric weight. If gross weight is higher, gross weight becomes the chargeable weight.

Why did my quotation change after warehouse measurement?

The cargo may have been measured differently, repacked, palletized, or confirmed to have higher volumetric weight than originally declared.

Can packaging reduce chargeable weight?

Yes, efficient packaging can reduce unnecessary volume. However, cargo must still be packed safely for air transport and handling.
