The Core Trade-off: Speed vs Cost

The fundamental difference between air and sea freight is simple: air is faster and more expensive, sea is slower and cheaper. But the right choice depends on your specific cargo, timeline, and budget.

Air Freight: Pros and Cons

Advantages:

  • Fast transit — 1–7 days worldwide
  • High security and less handling
  • Ideal for high-value, time-sensitive, or perishable goods
  • Real-time tracking
  • Lower insurance premiums

Disadvantages:

  • Higher cost per kg
  • Size and weight restrictions
  • Not suitable for hazardous liquids in large quantities

Sea Freight: Pros and Cons

Advantages:

  • Much lower cost for large, heavy shipments
  • No practical size/weight limits
  • Suitable for bulk commodities

Disadvantages:

  • Slow — 2–6 weeks transit time
  • Higher risk of damage and theft
  • Port congestion delays

When to Choose Air Freight

  • High-value goods (electronics, jewelry, pharmaceuticals)
  • Perishables (fresh produce, flowers, seafood)
  • Urgent or time-sensitive shipments
  • Small to medium shipments (under 500 kg typically)
  • E-commerce fulfillment

When to Choose Sea Freight

  • Heavy or bulky cargo over 500 kg
  • Non-urgent shipments
  • Commodities (timber, palm oil, rubber)
  • Full container loads (FCL)

Cost Comparison Example

Shipping 100 kg of textiles from Jakarta to Rotterdam:

  • Air freight: ~$600–900 USD (3–5 days)
  • Sea freight: ~$150–300 USD (25–35 days)

The price difference is significant, but if your goods are worth $50,000, the extra $600 for air freight is a small insurance premium for faster, safer delivery.
